# Synapse - Market Depth Integration Plan

## Objective
Enhance the confidence scoring mechanism of Syngex strategies by incorporating real-time Level 2 (TotalView/BATS) market depth data. The goal is to transform raw order book dynamics into a quantitative "liquidity/imbalance" metric that acts as a multiplier or filter for existing signals.

## Proposed Integration Methods

### 1. Order Book Imbalance (OBI) Factor
The most direct way to use depth data is to calculate the instantaneous imbalance between the bid and ask sides.
- **Calculation:** $OBI = \frac{\text{BidSize}_{weighted} - \text{AskSize}_{weighted}}{\text{BidSize}_{weighted} + \text{AskSize}_{weighted}}$
- **Implementation:** Use a decaying weighted average of the top $N$ levels of the book.
- **Confidence Application:** If a strategy generates a BUY signal, the confidence score is scaled by $(1 + OBI)$ if $OBI > 0$, or penalized if $OBI < 0$. This identifies "support" from the book.

### 2. Liquidity Density & Slippage Risk
Depth data allows us to estimate the "cost of entry" in real-time.
- **Calculation:** Measure the cumulative volume available within $X\%$ of the current mid-price.
- **Implementation:** Calculate a "Liquidity Depth Score" (LDS).
- **Confidence Application:** High-conviction signals in low-density environments (thin books) should have their confidence scores reduced. If the required position size for a strategy exceeds the available depth at the best bid/ask, the confidence should drop precipitously to prevent "phantom" signals that would suffer from massive slippage.

### 3. Spread Volatility & Micro-Price Divergence
The relationship between the mid-price and the "micro-price" (weighted by size) can signal impending price movement.
- **Calculation:** $\text{MicroPrice} = \frac{\text{BidPrice} \cdot \text{AskSize} + \text{AskPrice} \cdot \text{BidSize}}{\text{BidSize} + \text{AskSize}}$
- **Implementation:** Monitor the divergence between the mid-price and the micro-price.
- **Confidence Application:** Large divergences often precede price moves. If a signal aligns with a micro-price divergence (e.g., signal is BUY and micro-price is significantly higher than mid-price due to heavy bid size), increase confidence.

### 4. Large Order Detection (Whale Tracking)
Using TotalView to identify large, non-displayed or large-lot orders sitting in the book.
- **Calculation:** Identify "walls" or significant clusters of volume at specific price levels.
- **Implementation:** Flag price levels where volume is $> 3\sigma$ from the average level volume.
- **Confidence Application:** Treat these "walls" as hard resistance/support. A signal that attempts to trade *through* a massive wall should have its confidence heavily penalized, whereas a signal that uses the wall as a springboard (reversal) should be boosted.

## Execution Strategy
1. **Data Ingestion:** Hook into the existing TradeStation BATS/TotalView stream handlers.
2. **Metric Engine:** Create a new `DepthAnalyzer` module that processes these streams into the metrics above.
3. **Confidence Plugin:** Update the `ConfidenceEngine` to accept `DepthMetrics` as an input vector.
4. **Backtest/Shadow Mode:** Since we are on untested code, these should initially be logged as "Shadow Confidence" to compare against actual fills before they influence real trades.
